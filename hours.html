london

circa 1968

In 1968, London's food scene reflected the country's broader transition away from the rigid, frugal habits of the post-war years and towards a new era of culinary exploration. While the traditional "meat and two veg" and pub snacks like crisps and pickled eggs remained staples for many working-class families, the city also saw a rise in new tastes and dining experiences.

For most Londoners, however, dinner was still a home-cooked affair, centered on traditional, uncomplicated recipes. It was a time when cooking from scratch was the norm, and health food stores and restaurants, while existing, were still a niche counter-cultural novelty for many.

As the economy boomed and living standards rose, dinner parties became popular, offering a new social stage to showcase fashionable dishes and the newly accessible table wines. A new culture of food paved the way for Mr. Bates success.
